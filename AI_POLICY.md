# AI Policy

日本語版: [AI_POLICY.ja.md](AI_POLICY.ja.md)

Using AI (LLMs, coding agents) as tools for contributing is welcome. I use
them too: these repositories are maintained with the help of AI agents,
reviewed and accounted for by the maintainer. The same accountability is
expected from contributors.

## The rules

- **A human must be in the loop.** You are responsible for everything you
  submit. Fully autonomous drive-by contributions — where no human
  understands the change — will be closed, possibly without notice.
- **Explain it in your own words.** If you open an issue, you should be able
  to describe the problem yourself. If you open a pull request, you should be
  able to explain the change and answer review questions yourself. Do not
  paste AI responses as replies to maintainers.
- **Show your investigation.** For bug fixes, include evidence the problem
  exists (a failing test, logs, a reproduction) — not just an AI's claim
  that it does.
- **Disclose quoted AI output.** If you include AI-generated context in a
  discussion, put it in a quote block, label it, and add your own commentary
  on why it matters. Keep it short.
- **Translation is fine.** If English or Japanese is not your first language,
  using AI for translation is welcome. Consider writing in your native
  language and attaching the translation.

## Why

AI makes it cheap to produce plausible-looking changes and expensive to
review them. These rules keep the cost where it belongs: with the author,
not the reviewer.

This policy was inspired by the AI policies of
